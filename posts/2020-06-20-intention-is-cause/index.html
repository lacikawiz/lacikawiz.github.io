Intention is Cause

Jun 20, 2020
2 min read

Here’s a little fun story of my adventure today.

We ordered a printer online. Which didn’t come on time and only after some nudging I found out that it actually did arrive but the delivery company just forgot to inform us.

So, I came home for lunch. It’s Saturday, and just happen to think about picking up the printer. So I looked up the place on the map. And it said, it’s closing at 3pm and won’t open until Monday. Oh, oh. It’s 2:50pm. My wife is on a phone with a client which doesn’t seem to end anytime soon. I don’t speak much Russian and she said we might need her papers in order to pick up the printer because it was under her name.

I made a quick snap decision: what the hell, go for it. I had the messages from the delivery company on my phone with the order number and I thought that should be enough. I planned out the quickest way to the place (which I never visited before), grabbed the car key, ran down the 7 stories of stairs, jumped in the car and drove to the place. I got to the door at 2:59pm. A guy was already preparing to lock the door. I forgot how to say “pick up” in Russian, so I just said “order”. Then I successfully answered all the questions, and got our printer. They were not too happy but I don’t think I held them up more than 5 minutes.

I drove back home, smiling. Mission accomplished :) I get home, with the printer balanced on my shoulder. My wife is still on the phone with the client. Her eyes opened wide and jaw dropped, and just asked “Kak?” which means “How?”.

Well, this little story demonstrates the power of intention. With it almost anything can be accomplished (I personally believe given enough time, there’s nothing that is out of question), but without it almost nothing gets done.
