Independence Day

Jun 15, 2020
2 min read

Today is a my own personal Independence Day. Two years ago on this day, I started a new life.

What lead up to this point is that I had a dream of making a difference in the world & gave up everything for it. But after a year, I had to realize that every one of my goals were thoroughly blocked and I was mired down in rules, regulations - written, spoken and un-spoken ones. I’m a free spirit, so this suffocated me to a degree that I went to a deep apathy.

And when I hit bottom, I realized there’s no reason why I can’t just start a new life. So, I went through the process of shedding my shackles that I’ve accumulated. And, on June 15th, 2018 I stepped on a plane back to the US, and felt extremely liberated. Although, for the next several months I spent almost every waking moment on building up a new life from scratch, working hard, I felt I was on a vacation.

And my new life has been a huge success. I built it on the experience of the previous “lives”, avoiding many of the pitfalls that lead to the dead-ends and daily grind in the previous lives.

I’ve basically became a digital nomad - programming and making a living at anyplace I can find good internet connection. I love what I’m doing and I love learning new things, overcoming technical challenges. I’ve built a system (still under heavy development) that increased the productivity of a small business at least two-fold. And I did that while learning and experimenting with new technologies.

And in my personal life, I also succeeded quite well. I found the woman of my dreams, moved to Russia (being a digital nomad came very handy) to be with her and married her. After more than a year together our love for each other is still growing. One day, I’ll need to write down, in simple terms, how I did it. I would really love to see more happily married people.

If you are reading this, by some strange accident, and you are in a situation that looks dire and your dreams are blighted, my advice to you is to fully end your old life and start a new one. As one of the smartest person I know said:

“There is no person alive who cannot make a new beginning.” - L Ron Hubbard
