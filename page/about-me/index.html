About me

Jun 16, 2020
2 min read

I think I have a pretty tough time with this one.

We have good ways to describe objects in the physical universe but persons are trickier to do. The mind and spirit are pretty intangible entities. So, all I can do is to describe things about me.

I think the most important trait I have is that I am a seeker of knowledge. Pretty much everything interests me but I prefer to know things which are useful, have some kind of practical purpose.

I experiment a lot. I try new things all the time. I’m never satisfied with settling down and doing one thing one way always. This mentality is fit well with my profession as a programmer. And it doesn’t fit so well with some of the jobs I’ve tried in the past which required sticking to a rigid set of rules.

Another trait I have is thinking differently than pretty much anybody else. Everybody who I’ve been associated with told me this at some point or other. Sometimes using words such as “crazy”. However, here this word is not used in a sense of being senselessly destructive or antisocial. I’m the polar opposite of these things. Wherever I go, I work hard at improving things and I have friends all over the world who are always glad to see me or hear from me.

Because I think differently, I have a lot of invention ideas. I have a big one that I’ve been polishing for years now. It’s about re-thinking the whole world of software. I plan to post that here in the not too distant future. As soon as I came to grips with this Hugo system.

One of my guiding principles is simplicity. I found that whenever I encounter something that is more complicated than it needs to be then there are always ways to accomplish the same thing in a simpler way. In programming, this is especially useful. I keep looking for tools, frameworks, programming languages which make my work and production simpler. And I am very glad to see that things are moving in the direction of less complexity and more consistency, in general.
